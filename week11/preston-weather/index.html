Latest Forecast

Posted By: Sarah Jacobsen - Weather Anchor

Updated: Saturday, March 27, 2021, 2:15 p.m.

Happy Saturday! High pressure is taking over as we head into the weekend which means sunny skies and warming temperatures! Expect highs in the 60's on Saturday, in the low to mid 70's on Sunday! These will be the warmest temperatures of the season so far with the chance of beating a heat record on Sunday!

The warm-up is ahead of another strong cold front expected on Monday morning. A Wind Advisory is in effect Eastern Oregon and portions of SW Idaho. Winds of 25-35 mph are possible with gusts up to 50mph. Some valley rain and mountain snow showers will likely occur Monday with the front moving through. The biggest impact on the region will be cooler temperatures and blustery winds. Temperatures will be dropping 20-25 degrees from Sunday's highs, we will struggle to hit 50 degrees on Monday. The storm will clear out early Tuesday and temperatures will gradually warm through midweek. The forecast is calling for highs to be back near 70 degrees by Thursday. Stay tuned.

...WIND ADVISORY IN EFFECT FROM 1 AM MDT /MIDNIGHT PDT/ TO 6 AM MDT /5 AM PDT/ MONDAY...

* WHAT...West winds 25 to 35 mph with gusts up to 50 mph expected.

* WHERE...Portions of southeast Oregon and southwest Idaho.

* WHEN...From 1 AM MDT /midnight PDT/ to 6 AM MDT /5 AM PDT/ Monday.

* IMPACTS...Gusty winds could blow around unsecured objects. Tree limbs could be blown down and a few power outages may result.

PRECAUTIONARY/PREPAREDNESS ACTIONS... Use extra caution when driving, especially if operating a high profile vehicle. Secure outdoor objects.
