Issues:
1. How will Dr. Mobile integrate with customer insurance so that patients are not billed unnecessarily?

•    Contributing Variables:
- How likely is an insurance company not to pay?
- How severe are the injuries? If they are not severe, the insurance company may not pay anything. If they are severe, the insurance company is more likely to have to pay for more materials.

•   Mitigation: Prevention

o   If a situation is severe, begin contacting insurance company as early as possible.

o   Reach out to companies before the application is used to find out how much they will be willing to pay verses how severe the injury is.

•   Mitigation Test

o   Measurement: Percent of bills paid for by insurance when the app is used vs percent paid when the app is not used.

o   Test: At one hospital where the app is being adopted, record the cost of bills and how much is paid for by insurance, then compare that to the percent paid historically without the app. If the percent is better, it is successful. If it is not, investigate why.

•   Mitigation: Prevention

o   Patient data cannot be decided, but it can be organized well in the app. To prevent this risk, the app interface can be brought through many versions while presenting detailed prototypes to people who will be using them, and careful notes taken. Better prototypes require more time, but in this case an easy-to-use app is essential.

•   Mitigation test

o   Measurement: Number of incorrect entries in the app during a testing period.

o   Test: While making prototypes, get nurses, doctors, and other workers to test the app in a simulated environment using the app. Take notes of any comments they make on the interface or item naming.

•   Mitigation: Prevention

o   Give permission to view certain pieces of information only to authorized staff members. If a staff member does not need to see it, do not give them access. If a nurse is preparing you for a visit, they should be given access to basic medical information as well as the form that is typically given on paper.

•   Mitigation Test

o   Measurement: How much sensitive information is available to any staff with your profile?

o   Test: Check that any information that is not needed by staff is not available to them. If any data can be accessed without authorization, it has failed.

•   Mitigation: Acceptance

o   It is likely that some hospitals will reject this app because they have complex systems in place and transferring would be difficult. This is a risk that is inherent in developing software.

•   Mitigation Test

o   Measurement: What percent of medical staff adopts the tool? How satisfied are they?

o   Test: During the first six months of integration of the tool into daily work, keep track of the number of devices used and frequency of use and see if it goes up. Send out surveys to see what kind of response is generally given. If use decreases during the six months, the app is failing and may need more designing.
